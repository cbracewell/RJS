Wondering what counts?

Insurers need to know about any driving offences you’ve committed, such as Driving without due care and attention, or Driving with alcohol level above the limit. They also need to know about any Fixed Penalty Notices (FPNs) issued for offences such as speeding.

Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs) issued for parking offences do not have to be included, so you don’t have to mention these.

Please let us know if you have had any driving offences or fixed penalty notices in the last 5 years

Your insurance

Wondering how to find out?

Your years of no claims should be on your current insurance documents. It’s the number of years you can get in writing from your current insurance company.

Bear in mind that you can only use your no claims discount on one car or insurance policy at a time.

Some insurance companies won’t accept your no claims discount if you earned any or all of it on a van or a motorcycle, company car, fleet insurance or outside of the UK, so check before you buy.

Starting from scratch?

If this is your first insurance, or if you haven’t driven for a while, start with a zero.

What's the best option?

Paying annually means you’ll clear the bill for your whole year’s insurance today, and avoid any fees for paying monthly. Monthly payments mean you can spread the cost, but you’ll end up paying more in total.

Monthly payments are not available to people under 18, unless the credit agreement is with someone older, e.g. a parent is paying
